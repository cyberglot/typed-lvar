Terms ($\tm{\ell}$, $\tm{M}$, $\tm{N}$) are defined by the following grammar:
\[
  \begin{array}{lrll}
  \tm{\ell}, \tm{M}, \tm{N}
  & \coloneqq & \tm{x}
    \sep        \tm{\lambda x.M}
    \sep        \tm{M\;N}                     & \text{functions} \\
  & \sep      & \tm{\ell}
    \sep        \tm{d}
    \sep        \tmJ
    \sep        \tm{K}                        & \text{constants} \\
  & \sep      & \tm{\unit}
    \sep        \tm{\letunit{M}{N}}           & \text{units} \\
  & \sep      & \tm{\pair{M}{N}}
    \sep        \tm{\letpair{x}{y}{M}{N}}     & \text{pairs}
  \\
  \\
  \tm{K}
  & \coloneqq & \tm{\lvarnew}
    \sep        \tm{\lvarfreeze}
    \sep        \tm{\lvarget}
    \sep        \tm{\lvarput}
\end{array}
\]
